Games Development:
Year 2

Second year game development moved away from drag and drop interfaces such as GameMaker, and towards code based solutions. We began writing games in Python, using the Pygame API and exploring more advanced concept such as AI. Shown below are two sample games - both require Python 2.7 and Pygame to run.

Chistmas project - this is my Semester 1 project. It is a scrolling shooter, where the player must attack oncoming planes and dodge bullets fired by the enemies. Unzip and double click on Game/game.py to run.

End of year project - this is my Semester 2 project. It is a zombie survival game. The aim is simple; use your skills to survive endless waves of zombies and see how long you can last! You start off with a basic pistol, but as the waves of zombies get increasingly large you must look out for new weapons dropped by their previous owners. You have to act quick though - shortly after being dropped the weapons will disappear. The weapons all have a different feel and this game is complemented by a simple, yet effective, graphic and sound system.

Unzip and double click on Game/main.py to run.
